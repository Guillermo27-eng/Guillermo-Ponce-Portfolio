# Urban Resilience Index Analysis

Policy and GIS analysis evaluating climate resilience indicators for the Baldwin Hills & Urban Watersheds Conservancy (BHUWC).

This project examined how resilience categories, environmental indicators, and GIS mapping tools can be used to identify high-vulnerability communities and support equitable climate investment strategies across Los Angeles County.

The analysis focused on evaluating the accessibility, transparency, and comprehensiveness of environmental and resilience indicators while exploring how GIS-based planning tools can strengthen climate adaptation and public investment decision-making.

## Key Contributions
- Evaluated resilience indicators across multiple environmental and equity categories
- Conducted cross-comparison analysis of GIS-based resilience mapping tools
- Assessed accessibility, transparency, and comprehensiveness of resilience indicators
- Analyzed environmental burden indicators including pollution exposure, hazardous facilities, impervious surfaces, and public health sensitivity risks
- Developed recommendations to improve usability and community accessibility of the Urban Resilience Index
- Connected resilience planning analysis to California Proposition 4 climate investment opportunities and infrastructure planning
