# From Poverty to Gang Involvement

Policy analysis examining systemic poverty, intergenerational trauma, institutional distrust, and gang vulnerability among youth in Los Angeles County.

This project explored how historical structural inequalities and community disinvestment contribute to long-term social and public safety outcomes while evaluating intervention strategies, trauma-informed programs, and prevention-focused policy recommendations.

## Focus Areas
- Social Policy
- Criminal Justice Policy
- Youth Development
- Trauma-Informed Policy
- Community-Based Research

## Tools Used
- Policy Analysis
- Research Synthesis
- Cost-Benefit Analysis
- Program Evaluation
- Presentation Design

## Key Contributions
- Examined the relationship between poverty, trauma, and gang involvement
- Evaluated intervention programs including GRYD and trauma-focused therapy models
- Developed policy recommendations at the micro, mezzo, and macro levels
- Conducted cost-benefit analysis for prevention-focused interventions
- Connected structural inequality to long-term institutional distrust and violence prevention challenges
